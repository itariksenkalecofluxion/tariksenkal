What is RAG, and why does it matter?

TŞ
İsmail Tarık Şenkal
EcoFluxion · Co-Founder
· 6 min read

RAG stands for “Retrieval-Augmented Generation.” It sounds complicated, but the idea is remarkably simple — and almost vital for those of us working in legal technology.

“First find the relevant documents, then write the answer by looking at those documents.”

The problem it solves

A large language model is limited to the data it was trained on, and it can sometimes “make up” wrong information with great confidence (hallucination). Giving a lawyer the wrong Court of Cassation (Yargıtay) decision number? Unacceptable. RAG exists precisely to prevent this.

How does it work? (The open-book exam analogy)

Picture two kinds of students:

  • The rote-memorizing student (a plain LLM): Sits the exam relying solely on memory. Knows most things, but makes things up when memory fails.
  • The open-book student (RAG): Reads the question, first finds the relevant page in the source material, then writes the answer by looking at that page. Gets it right and can cite the source.

RAG is the second student. Step by step:

  1. A question comes in. For example, “case law based on an undertaking to vacate.”
  2. Retrieval. The system finds the most semantically relevant documents in a vector database — matching by meaning, not by keyword.
  3. Generation. The LLM writes the answer by looking at these real documents it found, and ties it back to the source.
The result: Answers are anchored to reality. The model now speaks not from “memory” but from the documents placed before it. This both improves accuracy and makes it possible to answer the question “where did you find this?”

Why is semantic search essential?

The heart of RAG is the “find the relevant document” step. Classic keyword search looks for the exact word “eviction”; yet the user may have said “removal from the home.” Semantic search matches meaning rather than words — which is why vector databases are used. Texts are converted into numerical “vectors” that represent their meaning, and those that are close in meaning are found quickly.

Where it fits in our work

The secret behind İçtiHub's reliability is, to a large extent, RAG. Through RAG, we combine our model trained for the Turkish legal language (MevzuatBot) with a real archive of case law and legislation. As a result, the legal professional gets answers that are both fast and source-backed — that is, trustworthy.

In short, RAG transforms artificial intelligence from a “daydreaming” narrator into a “source-citing” researcher. In fields like law, that is exactly where the difference lies. You can read about why this idea matters especially for Turkish in the article “Why Turkish AI?”.
