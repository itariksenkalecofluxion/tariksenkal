Why Turkish AI?

TŞ
İsmail Tarık Şenkal
EcoFluxion · Co-Founder
· 6 min read

There is a fact that is often overlooked when people talk about artificial intelligence: nearly all of the most powerful models are trained predominantly on English text. For more than 80 million Turkish speakers, this amounts to a silent disadvantage. That was precisely our starting point when we founded EcoFluxion.

“The value of a technology is measured by whether it actually works in the native language of the people who need it most.”

Why is Turkish a “hard” language?

From an AI standpoint, Turkish carries challenges all its own:

  • Agglutinative structure: Dozens of suffixes can attach to a single root (“ev → evlerimizden”). This makes the number of word forms explode and puts a strain on the model.
  • Different syntax: Sentence construction is quite different from English.
  • Scarce resources: High-quality, labeled Turkish data is not as abundant as it is for English.
  • Domain language: The Turkish terminology of fields like law, medicine, and the public sector is highly specialized.

A general-purpose model often stumbles over these subtleties — especially in fields like law, where the margin for error needs to be low.

This is not just about “translation.” Translating Turkish into English and then processing it distorts the meaning and the legal nuance. The right path is to train models that understand Turkish directly.

Our approach

At EcoFluxion, we try to close this gap across three layers:

  1. Models built for Turkish: Rather than borrowing a general-purpose model, we train our own language models, fine-tuned for Turkish and for Turkish legal language.
  2. Grounding in fact: With RAG, we anchor answers to real Turkish documents — to legislation and case law.
  3. Turning it into a product: We turn this into not a research demo but a product that legal professionals open every day: İçtiHub.

Why is it a matter of conviction?

Because AI will be the infrastructure of the coming decade. If that infrastructure works flawlessly only in English, then a Turkish-speaking lawyer, a student, or a civil servant will always be forced to settle for a second-class experience. We are not willing to accept that. Our goal is for every Turkish speaker to be able to access powerful, accurate, and trustworthy artificial intelligence in their own native language.

Turkish AI is not a luxury but a necessity. To see how this vision is taking shape as a company and as products, you can take a look at the story of EcoFluxion and What is İçtiHub?
